# 👥 HR Analytics Dashboard

## 📌 Project Overview
This project analyzes employee data across departments, performance, satisfaction, and recruitment sources to support HR decision-making. Built entirely using **Power BI**, covering employee records from 2006 to 2018.

---

## 🛠️ Tool Used
- Power BI — Data cleaning (Power Query), modeling & visualization

---

## 🧹 Data Cleaning Process (Power Query)
- ✅ Transformed and standardized date formats for accurate year-based filtering and analysis
- ✅ Cleaned and structured raw employee fields to ensure consistency across departments, marital status, and performance ratings

---

## 📊 Dashboard Overview
- Total Headcount — 311
- Active Employees — 207 (66.56%)
- Terminated Employees — 104 (33.44%)
- Male — 135 (43.41%)
- Female — 176 (56.59%)

---

## 💡 Key Findings
- 🏭 **Production is the largest department** with 209 employees, far ahead of IT/IS (50) and Sales (31)
- ⭐ **78.14% of employees "Fully Meet" performance expectations**, while only 4.18% fall under PIP (Performance Improvement Plan)
- 💍 **Single employees make up the largest share** at 44.05%, followed closely by Married employees at 39.87%
- 📋 **Indeed and LinkedIn are the top recruitment sources**, contributing 137 and 76 hires respectively
- 😊 **34.73% of employees rate satisfaction as "Acceptable"**, while 30.23% rate it "Very High"
- 💰 **Production also leads in total salary allocation** at 12.5M, more than double every other department combined
